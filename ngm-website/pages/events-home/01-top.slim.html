Beyond the retreat

Other ways to gather

The Guild offers special programs that fall outside the regular monthly meetings — workshops and lectures with nationally known instructors, seasonal getaways, and more.

Traditional Japanese Embroidery

Two workshops a year with Kay Stanis, certified by Kurenai Kai. Up to six phases are taught at once, but each must be taken in order. Open to anyone interested.

Workshops & Lectures

Sessions with nationally known instructors throughout the year, teaching new techniques and projects. Recent and upcoming teachers include Catherine Theron.

5th Tuesday Open Stitching

A bonus open-stitching session held on months that have a fifth Tuesday. Bring any project and stitch alongside fellow members — no program, just company.

March to the Finish

A winter getaway and stitch-in — dedicated time to make progress on projects, away from everyday distractions, in good company.

A Fall Finish

A seasonal stitching event to wrap up projects as the year winds down — a relaxed day of finishing and fellowship.

Outreach Activities

Community outreach that shares the craft beyond the Guild — bringing needlework to new audiences and giving back.
