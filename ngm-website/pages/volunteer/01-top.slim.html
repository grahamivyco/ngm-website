Get involved

Volunteer opportunities

The Needlework Guild of Minnesota runs entirely on member volunteers — there’s no paid staff behind the scenes. Whatever your interest or the time you can spare, there’s a role that fits. Lend a hand once, or lead a group for the year.

Roles & positions

Where members step up

These are the volunteer roles that keep the Guild running from month to month. Some are elected board positions, others are ongoing jobs — every one of them is filled by a member like you.

President

Leads the board, sets the direction for the year, and runs the annual meeting. The public face of the Guild and the person who keeps everything moving.

Vice President

Supports the President and steps in when needed. Often takes the lead on special projects and helps coordinate committees through the year.

Treasurer

Keeps the Guild’s books — dues, expenses, and the annual budget. Handles deposits and reimbursements and reports the finances to the board.

Secretary

Takes minutes at board and membership meetings and keeps the Guild’s official records. A great role for someone who likes to keep things organized.

Events & Programs

Plans the year’s programs and guest speakers, books instructors, and lines up the workshops that members look forward to. Creative and hands-on.

Sunday Blast Editor

Writes and sends the weekly member email — meeting reminders, program news, and announcements. A steady, behind-the-scenes way to keep everyone in the loop.

Advertising

Coordinates advertisers and sponsor listings, and helps get the word out about Guild events to the wider stitching community.

Website Manager

Keeps the website current — updating pages, posting events, and making sure members can find what they need. Comfort with editing web pages helps.

Website Communications

Gathers news and updates from around the Guild and turns them into content for the website. Works closely with the Website Manager.

Librarian

Cares for the Guild’s lending library — checking books in and out, keeping the catalog current, and helping members find their next project reference.

Shop Manager

Runs the Guild shop — managing stock of threads, kits, and supplies, and helping members with purchases at meetings and events.

Retreat Chair

Organizes the annual retreat from start to finish — venue, instructors, registration, and logistics. A big, rewarding job, usually shared by a small team.

Daytime Stitch-In Leader

Hosts the daytime Stitch-In — welcoming members, opening the space, and keeping the gathering warm and running smoothly each month.

Evening Stitch-In Leader

Hosts the evening Stitch-In for members who stitch after hours — setting up, greeting everyone, and keeping the evening group going.

No commitment needed

Ways to pitch in

Not ready to take on a role? There are plenty of small ways to help that take just a few minutes — and they make a real difference at every meeting.

Set up & clean up

Arrive a little early or stay a few minutes late to help arrange chairs and tables, or tidy the room once the stitching wraps.

Bring treats

Sign up to bring a snack or beverage for a meeting. A little hospitality goes a long way toward making everyone feel at home.

Greet new members

Say hello to first-time visitors, help them find a seat, and introduce them around. A friendly welcome is what keeps people coming back.

Help in the library

Lend a hand at the library table during meetings — checking books in and out and helping members browse the collection.

Lend a hand

Interested in helping?

Tell us what you enjoy and how much time you have — we’ll help you find a role that fits. Every hour our members give is what keeps the Guild going.
