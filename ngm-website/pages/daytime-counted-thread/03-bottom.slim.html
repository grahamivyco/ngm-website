What we explore

Counted techniques, every month

We gather the 2nd Tuesday of each month, 10 am to 2 pm — in person in Bloomington or by video. Each meeting centers on a program or demonstration, then time to stitch and share, and programs alternate monthly between counted thread and needlepoint. Whether you love the precision of counted thread or the texture of canvas needlepoint, there's something here, and you're always welcome to bring your own project instead. Every skill level is welcome, whether you're just curious or have been stitching for years.

  • Cross stitch
  • Hardanger
  • Pulled thread
  • Drawn thread
  • Blackwork
  • Hemstitching
  • Canvas needlepoint

Good to know

Common questions

When and where does this group meet?
The 2nd Tuesday of each month, 10 AM to 2 PM. It's hybrid — join in person at Minnesota Valley Unitarian Universalist Fellowship in Bloomington, or by video.
Do I have to follow the program?
No. Programs alternate between counted thread and needlepoint, but you're always welcome to bring your own project instead.
What skill level do I need?
Any. All skill levels are welcome, from curious beginners to experienced stitchers.
How do I get the video link?
The videoconference link is on the Member Resources page — log in to access it. Trouble connecting? Email inquiry@needleworkguildmn.org.
