The tradition

An age-old art

Traditional Japanese embroidery is a centuries-old practice of silk and metallic threads worked on silk. Guild member and Kurenai Kai–certified teacher Kay Stanis leads two workshops a year, guiding students through the phases of the curriculum at their own pace.

  • Silk & metallic threads
  • Six phases
  • Taken in order
  • Open to all
